Head First Design Patterns: A Brain-Friendly Guide

In this post I would like to talk about an interesting book that I certainly recommend to developers: Head First Design Patterns. Here are some of the reasons:
First of all, the narrative style is particular and fun. With the use of sarcasm, jokes, drawings..etc, the authors believe that if you experience emotions while you are reading their book, then your brain will be more receptive and you’ll retain information better. Note that it’s a different method of learning. That happened to me, when I think about a design pattern such as the strategy pattern, I recall the example of the ducks and their different flying behaviors, or the weather sensor station with the observer pattern with their class diagrams, concepts and code. Another important reason to love this book is because you truly learn easily and it helps in your professional career. I still remember my first job interview when some questions were about design patterns, this books helped me in order to explain a couple of points.

In conclusion, here are my ‘bullet points’ of what to expect from this book:

  • Learn design patterns used in the industry Such as strategy pattern, observer pattern, factory method, abstract factory pattern…
  • Examples and concepts easy to learn and remember
  • The pre-requisite is to have experience programming. This book will not teach you how to code, instead I would recommend you other books like Head First Java development.
  • The principles can be applied to other programming languages, not just Java.
